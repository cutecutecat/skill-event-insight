Charging Options

Home Charging

The most convenient way to charge is right at home. Simply plug in when you arrive and wake up to a full battery every morning. Most VoltDrive owners do 80% of their charging at home.

Level 1 - Standard Outlet

Use the included Mobile Connector with any standard 120V household outlet. Adds approximately 3-5 miles of range per hour. Best for overnight charging with shorter daily commutes.

  • No installation required
  • Included with every VoltDrive
  • ~40 miles overnight (12 hours)

Level 2 - Wall Connector

Our Wall Connector installs in your garage and delivers up to 44 miles of range per hour. This is the recommended solution for most owners, providing a full charge overnight regardless of daily driving.

  • Up to 44 miles of range per hour
  • Full charge in 8-10 hours
  • Sleek, weather-resistant design
  • Wi-Fi connectivity for monitoring

Wall Connector: $475 | Professional installation: $500-1,500

Supercharger Network

Our proprietary Supercharger network spans over 45,000 chargers worldwide. Strategically placed along major highways and in urban centers, Superchargers make long-distance travel effortless.

V3 Supercharger

Our latest Supercharger technology delivers up to 250 kW of power, adding up to 200 miles of range in just 15 minutes. Liquid-cooled cables are lightweight and easy to handle.

  • Up to 250 kW charging speed
  • 200 miles in 15 minutes
  • No power sharing between stalls
  • Automatic billing through the app

V4 Supercharger (Coming Soon)

Our next-generation Superchargers will deliver up to 350 kW, with longer cables that can reach vehicles parked on either side. The first V4 stations are now being installed across North America and Europe.

Charging Best Practices

Daily Charging Limit

For daily use, set your charge limit to 80%. This preserves battery longevity while still providing plenty of range. Only charge to 100% before long trips.

Scheduled Charging

Take advantage of off-peak electricity rates by scheduling charging to start late at night. The car will automatically begin charging at your specified time.

Precondition Before Charging

In cold weather, precondition your battery before arriving at a Supercharger. The car will automatically do this if you navigate to a Supercharger using the built-in navigation.

Avoid Frequent DC Fast Charging

While occasional Supercharging is perfectly fine, relying exclusively on DC fast charging can slightly accelerate battery degradation. Home charging is gentler on the battery.

Charging FAQ

How long does it take to charge a VoltDrive?

It depends on your charging method. At home with a Wall Connector, expect a full charge in 8-10 hours. At a Supercharger, you can add 200 miles of range in about 15 minutes.

Can I charge in the rain?

Yes! VoltDrive vehicles and charging equipment are designed to be completely safe in wet conditions. The charging port and connectors meet IP67 waterproof standards.

What happens if I run out of charge?

VoltDrive provides multiple warnings as your battery level decreases. The navigation system will automatically suggest nearby chargers. If you do run out, roadside assistance can bring a mobile charger to you.

Is charging cheaper than gas?

Significantly! The average cost to charge a VoltDrive is equivalent to about $1.00-1.50 per gallon of gas. Home charging during off-peak hours can be even cheaper.
