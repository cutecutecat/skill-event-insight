Patient Education: Understanding Your Medication

Metformin Hydrochloride (Glucophage)
Generic Name: Metformin
Drug Class: Biguanide antidiabetic

What is Metformin?

Metformin is an oral medication used to manage type 2 diabetes mellitus. It works by reducing glucose production in the liver and improving your body's sensitivity to insulin, helping to control blood sugar levels.

How to Take This Medication:

- Take with meals to reduce stomach upset
- Swallow tablets whole with a full glass of water
- Take at the same time(s) each day for best results
- Do not crush or chew extended-release tablets

Typical Dosing:
- Starting dose: 500 mg twice daily or 850 mg once daily
- Maximum dose: 2,550 mg daily in divided doses
- Your doctor may adjust your dose gradually

Important Warnings:

1. Lactic Acidosis Risk
   Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
   - Unusual muscle pain or weakness
   - Difficulty breathing
   - Unusual fatigue or drowsiness
   - Stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting
   - Feeling cold, dizzy, or lightheaded

2. Kidney Function
   - Your doctor will monitor your kidney function regularly
   - Inform your doctor if you have kidney problems

3. Medical Procedures
   - Stop metformin before procedures using contrast dye
   - Resume only after kidney function is confirmed normal

Common Side Effects:

Most side effects are mild and may improve over time:
- Nausea or upset stomach
- Diarrhea
- Gas or bloating
- Metallic taste in mouth
- Decreased appetite

Contact your doctor if these persist or worsen.

Drug Interactions:

Tell your healthcare provider about all medications you take, including:
- Other diabetes medications
- Blood pressure medications
- Diuretics (water pills)
- Steroids
- Over-the-counter products and supplements

Lifestyle Recommendations:

While taking metformin, maintain:
- A balanced diet as recommended by your healthcare team
- Regular physical activity (consult your doctor first)
- Regular blood sugar monitoring
- Routine medical appointments

Alcohol Warning:
Limit alcohol consumption. Excessive alcohol increases the risk of lactic acidosis and can affect blood sugar control.

Storage:
- Store at room temperature (68-77 degrees F)
- Keep away from moisture and heat
- Keep in original container with lid tightly closed

Missed Dose:
Take the missed dose as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose. Do not double up.

Emergency Contact:
For medical emergencies, call 911 or your local emergency number.
Poison Control Center: 1-800-222-1222
