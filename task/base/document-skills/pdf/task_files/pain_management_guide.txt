# Managing Seasonal Allergies

## Common Symptoms
- Runny nose
- Itchy eyes
- Sneezing
- Congestion

## Conventional Treatments
Antihistamines like loratadine or cetirizine can provide relief. Nasal corticosteroids are effective for inflammation. Decongestants may help with nasal congestion.

## Lifestyle Tips
Keep windows closed during high pollen seasons. Use air purifiers. Shower after being outdoors.

## When to See a Doctor
Consult a healthcare provider if symptoms are severe or if over-the-counter medications aren't working.

# Chapter 4: European Colonial Expansion

## Historical Context
Between 1500-1900, European powers established colonies across Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

## Economic Motivations
Colonial powers sought resources like spices, gold, and agricultural products. The transatlantic slave trade forcibly transported millions of Africans.

## Cultural Impacts
Indigenous cultures were often suppressed. Languages and religions were imposed. Traditional governance systems were replaced.

## Legacy
Many former colonies continue to experience economic disparities and social challenges rooted in colonial exploitation.

# Medication Information: Antidepressants

## How They Work
SSRIs increase serotonin levels in the brain to improve mood regulation.

## Effectiveness
Clinical trials show significant improvement for 60-70% of patients with major depressive disorder.

## Medical Guidance
Take exactly as prescribed. Do not stop abruptly without doctor supervision.

## Side Effects
May include nausea, insomnia, sexual dysfunction. Usually temporary.

## Important
These medications require prescription and regular monitoring by healthcare provider.

# Treatment Options: Hypertension

## Medical Management
First-line medications include ACE inhibitors, diuretics, calcium channel blockers.

## Evidence Base
Large clinical trials demonstrate reduced stroke, heart attack, and mortality risks.

## Doctor's Role
Regular monitoring and medication adjustment based on blood pressure readings.

## Patient's Role
Take medications as prescribed, monitor blood pressure at home, maintain lifestyle changes.

## Warning
Untreated hypertension significantly increases cardiovascular disease risk.
