Key Points: Renewable Energy Advancements in 2024

The article examines major developments across the renewable energy sector in 2024, highlighting both progress and remaining challenges.

Solar technology has made substantial efficiency gains, with perovskite-silicon tandem cells now achieving 33.7% efficiency in commercial settings - a notable improvement that should reduce costs by 20% by 2026. Manufacturing remains concentrated in China (80%), though diversification efforts are underway in the US, India, and EU, partly driven by the US Inflation Reduction Act's $50 billion investment stimulus.

Wind power, particularly offshore, is experiencing rapid growth. The UK's Dogger Bank project exemplifies this trend at 3.6 GW capacity. Turbine technology has advanced to 15 MW units with 260-meter rotors, bringing offshore wind costs down to $50-60/MWh.

Battery storage has become essential infrastructure, with global capacity tripling since 2021 to 150 GWh. The shift from NMC to LFP chemistry reflects priorities around cost, safety, and longevity. Sodium-ion technology offers a path away from lithium dependence, while long-duration solutions like iron-air batteries address multi-day storage needs.

Green hydrogen costs have dropped to $3-4/kg, enabling early industrial adoption in steel manufacturing and shipping.

Investment reached $500 billion globally, creating 14 million jobs. Carbon pricing now covers 23% of emissions.

However, challenges persist: grid infrastructure limitations, lengthy permitting processes, supply chain vulnerabilities, and the ongoing debate about managing intermittency versus maintaining baseload capacity.

The sector's trajectory suggests the energy transition is outpacing expectations, though sustained effort remains necessary for 2050 net-zero goals.
